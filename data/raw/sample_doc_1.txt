In 2026, the most significant shift in machine learning is the transition from passive models to Agentic AI and GraphRAG (Graph Retrieval-Augmented Generation). Unlike traditional LLMs that simply predict the next token, agentic systems are designed to autonomously plan, execute multi-step workflows, and use external tools to achieve complex goals without constant human prompting. To support this, RAG has evolved into a "knowledge runtime" where vector databases are integrated with knowledge graphs to provide deep reasoning and relationship-mapping that simple semantic search often misses. Furthermore, there is a massive industry pivot toward Small Language Models (SLMs) that offer near-frontier performance at a fraction of the compute cost, enabling on-device AI and "Federated Learning" where models are trained locally on private data without ever sharing the raw information. This era is also defined by the EU AI Act, which has pushed Explainable AI (XAI) to the forefront, requiring models to be as transparent as they are capable
